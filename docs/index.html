Block distracting websites

Social media can be great, but it can also be a huge distraction when you need to study. Use this extension to temporarily block access to these sites to keep you focused on what's most important.

Get break reminders and helful notifications

Breaks are important for your mental and physical health. Reminders ensure you take these breaks and stay productive as well as remind you to stay healthy by going for a walk or drinking water.

Watch meditation and yoga tutorials

Meditation and yoga have been proven to improve both mental and physical health. Use study breaks to clear your mind and stay healthy.

Music

Play relaxing background music while you study to reduce stress and help increase focus.

Find healthy recipes

It can be hard to eat healthy while you're stressed and busy studying. Easy, healthy recipes that are perfect for student are provided.

Set timers to keep you on track

Try the Pomodoro method! Set the timer in the popup window to give you notifications every 25 minutes at which point you take a 5 minute break. Scale the frequency of the notifications to match your work habits.

Our Vision

Our team's goal is to create a tool to help reduce the stressors of studying and school for students. We realized that while there are several apps designed to block distracting websites and help create a study regimine, there were none that had all tools in one place.

As such, we built MERA Study. The browser extension allows students to turn on a website blocker to keep them on track. Helpful break reminders keep students productive and provide tips to reduce stress, such as by trying meditation and yoga.

These pages provide helpful links to meditation and yoga tutorials, as well as music that can be helpful while studying. Additionally, there are healthy recipes provided to encourage students to be healthy, even when stress is high.

By encouraging students to work to reduce stress, students are less likely to have stress related health issues that could result in hospitalization. By reducing the number of people in the hospital, the wait times to see doctors for everyone will be reduced. This will have a net positive impact on society.
